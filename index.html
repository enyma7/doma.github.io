Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to questions you should have asked yourself instead.

Q: Why was my Form 77-B rejected?
A: Everyone's Form 77-B is rejected. This is not personal. This is universal. The form exists to make you realize you don't need our approval.
Q: How long will processing take?
A: How long is a piece of string? How heavy is a thought? Your application is being processed at the speed of bureaucracy, which is to say, it is both moving and not moving simultaneously.
Q: Where can I find Form 77-C?
A: Form 77-C is out of print. It has been since 1987. Some say it never existed. Some say it exists in a filing cabinet on Sub-level 7. Sub-level 7 does not exist. Neither does the filing cabinet. Neither do you, really, if you think about it.
Q: What if I don't have a purpose?
A: Congratulations! You've reached enlightenment. You may now either: (a) create your own purpose, (b) live without purpose, or (c) fill out more forms. Most people choose (c). We don't know why.
Q: Is the Department real?
A: Define "real." We process forms. You submit forms. Something is happening here. Whether it's "real" is above our pay grade. (We're not paid.)
Q: Can you just tell me what the meaning of life is?
A: No. Next question.
Q: Why does the Meaning Calculator always return ERROR?
A: Because meaning cannot be calculated. It must be lived. Also, our IT budget was cut in 1993. Also, there is no IT department. Also, there is no department.
Q: What happens if I give up?
A: You are free. You always were. The forms were optional. Life is optional. Not existing is not an option we recommend, but the choice to engage with meaning or not is entirely yours.
Q: Who runs this department?
A: No one. Everyone. A philosophy grad student started it in 1987. They're probably dead now. Or they never existed. Either way, it continues. Like consciousness. Like bureaucracy. Like the boulder rolling up the hill.
